## Section 3: Distribution Strategy

### Channels Under Consideration

Cakewalk is evaluating four primary distribution channels, each with distinct trade-offs.

---

### Channel 1: Re-sellers (Trusted Advisors)

**Model:** Enable insurance agents to sell Cakewalk to their SMB clients

**How it works:**
- Agents have existing SMB relationships
- Cakewalk provides platform to quote/sell
- Agents earn commission, Cakewalk gets distribution

**Pros:**
- Leverages existing trust relationships
- Agents have sales skills
- Lower CAC than direct acquisition
- Agents motivated by commission

**Cons:**
- Creates dependency on intermediaries
- Potential conflict with "no brokers" brand promise
- Agents may not prioritize Cakewalk
- Training and enablement overhead

---

### Channel 2: Affinity Groups

**Model:** Partner with SMB networks (industry associations, chambers of commerce, franchises) to aggregate demand

**How it works:**
- Partner with groups representing many SMBs
- Offer group rates or exclusive access
- Group promotes to members, Cakewalk fulfills

**Pros:**
- Built-in trust (members trust the group)
- Aggregated demand (many SMBs at once)
- Potential for group pricing leverage
- Lower CAC per customer

**Cons:**
- Long partnership sales cycles
- Revenue share expectations
- Varied engagement across groups
- May require customization per group

---

### Channel 3: Embedded Partnerships

**Model:** Integrate into platforms SMBs already use (HRIS, payroll, accounting)

**How it works:**
- Native integration into partner platform
- SMB discovers Cakewalk inside existing workflow
- Seamless data flow, one-click enrollment

**Pros:**
- Meets SMBs where they already are
- High trust (partner vouches for you)
- Frictionless experience (pre-filled data)
- Sticky (hard to switch once embedded)
- Scales with partner's growth

**Cons:**
- Long integration timelines
- Partner has leverage (rev share, exclusivity)
- Dependent on partner's roadmap
- Requires dedicated engineering resources

---

### Channel 4: Book Acquisition Model (Strategic Focus)

**Model:** Acquire books of business from agents, but own the customer relationship entirely

**The Concept:**

Traditional agent model:
```
Agent finds customer → Agent sells → Agent services → Agent renews
(Agent involved forever)
```

Cakewalk model:
```
Agent sells access to book → Cakewalk acquires customer → Cakewalk owns relationship
(Agent exits after handoff)
```

**Why This Model:**
- Aligns with "no brokers" brand promise (customer never interacts with agent)
- Leverages existing distribution without ongoing dependency
- Clean economics: one-time CAC, full LTV ownership
- Solves agent's problem (can't profitably serve small accounts)

**Three Variations:**

**Option A: Buy the Book Outright**
- Pay agent one-time fee for right to market to their book
- Agent makes warm intro, then exits
- No trailing commissions

**Option B: Pay for Warm Introductions**
- Agent introduces Cakewalk to clients
- Paid only for successful conversions
- Customer experience is 100% Cakewalk

**Option C: Acquire Retiring Agent Books**
- Many agents aging out (average age ~59)
- Acquire entire books from retiring agents
- One-time purchase, full ownership transfer

**Target Agent Profiles:**
- Retiring agents looking to exit
- Agents with unprofitable small-account books
- Agents who want to focus on larger accounts

**Key Success Factors:**
- Smooth customer transition process
- Agent endorsement during handoff
- Quality assessment of acquired books
- Legal/compliance path for agent of record transfer

**Economics Example:**

| Model | Cost per Customer | LTV:CAC |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| $300 referral fee | $300 | 12.5:1 |
| $500 per enrollment | $500 | 7.5:1 |
| Buy book at 1x commission | $750 | 5:1 |
| Buy book at 2x commission | $1,500 | 2.5:1 |

*Assumes: $5K avg premium, 15% take rate, 5-year customer lifetime, $3,750 LTV*

---

### Product-Led Growth (PLG) Opportunity

**Goal:** Trigger SMB-to-SMB network effects without intermediaries

**PLG Mechanics to Build:**

**Mechanic 1: Employee-to-Employer Spread**
- Employees experience Cakewalk through enrollment
- When they start a business or join a new company, they recommend Cakewalk
- Enable with: shareable benefits cards, "refer your employer" program

**Mechanic 2: SMB Owner Networks**
- SMB owners share recommendations with each other
- Enable with: referral program, social proof, founder community

**Mechanic 3: Accountant/Bookkeeper Flywheel**
- Accountants serve multiple SMBs
- One happy client leads to recommendations to others
- Enable with: accountant partner program, multi-client dashboard

**Mechanic 4: Viral Product Features**
- "Invite your employees" (natural viral loop)
- Shareable benefits summaries
- Benchmarking tools
- Open enrollment reminders

**Mechanic 5: "Powered by Cakewalk" Effect**
- Every employee sees Cakewalk branding on benefits cards, emails, portal
- Creates passive awareness at scale

## Section 4: Pricing Strategy

**Two-Part Model:**

| Product Category | Pricing Model | Rate |
|------------------|---------------|------|
| Medical/Health | PEPM (flat fee) | $40 per employee per month |
| Ancillary (Dental, Vision, Life, Disability, Accident, Critical Care) | % of Premium | 25-27% |

*Note: $40 PEPM is pure commission/revenue — no carrier payments on top.*

---

### Product Suite

**Available Now:**
- Medical/Health (ACA and Non-ACA compliant)
- Dental
- Vision
- Life Insurance
- Disability (LTD/STD)
- Accident
- Critical Care (Cancer)

**Future:**
- 401(k)

**Packaging:**
- High/low plan options within product categories

### Pricing Risks & Mitigations

**Risk 1: Solo/1-Person Accounts Unprofitable**

Only $72.50/month revenue with $815 max CAC.

*Mitigations:*
- Accept as lead-gen (they may grow)
- Consider minimum monthly fee ($50-75 floor)
- Prioritize acquisition of 3+ employee accounts

**Risk 2: Medical-Only Customers (No Ancillary)**

- Revenue: $200/month (5 employees)
- ACV: $2,400
- LTV (5yr): $9,000
- Max CAC (4:1): $2,250

*Mitigations:*
- Bundle pricing incentives
- Upsell ancillary during onboarding
- Track ancillary attach rate as key metric

**Risk 3: PEPM Doesn't Scale with Inflation**

Flat $40 PEPM doesn't increase as premiums rise.

*Mitigations:*
- Annual price increases built into terms
- Revisit model as you scale

## Section 5: Sales & Customer Journey

### Journey Overview

```
DISCOVER → EXPLORE → ENROLL → ACTIVATE → RETAIN → EXPAND
```

---

### Stage 1: Discover

**How they find Cakewalk:**

| Channel | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Organic Search | Googling "small business health insurance" |
| Partner Platform | Discovery inside Gusto, payroll, HRIS |
| Referral | Another SMB owner recommends |
| Agent/Advisor | Warm introduction from trusted advisor |
| Affinity Group | Promotion through industry association |

**First Impression Goal:** "This looks simple and made for me."

---

### Stage 2: Explore

**Actions:**
1. Visit website
2. Window shop (browse plans, see pricing)
3. Sign up / create account

**Experience:**
- No commitment required to browse
- Transparent pricing visible upfront
- Account creation is lightweight

**Goal:** Reduce friction to "just looking."

---

### Stage 3: Enroll (Employer)

**Flow:**

```
Step 1: Tell us about yourself (owner)
           ↓
Step 2: Tell us about your business
           ↓
Step 3: Cakewalk generates recommendations
           ↓
Step 4: Owner reviews and tweaks
           ↓
Step 5: Confirm and enroll
```

**Experience:**
- Fully self-serve
- Human escalation available for edge cases
- Guided, opinionated flow (smart defaults)

**Target Time:** Minutes (not hours, not days)

---

### Stage 4: Activate (Employees)

**Flow:**

```
Step 1: Owner sets open enrollment window (up to 1 week)
           ↓
Step 2: Employees receive invite
           ↓
Step 3: Employees select their coverage (self-serve, guided)
           ↓
Step 4: Benefits become active
           ↓
Step 5: Cards/docs delivered
```

**Experience:**
- Owner manages open enrollment through platform
- Employees have self-serve, guided experience
- Target enrollment window: 1 week

---

### Stage 5: Retain

**What keeps them:**
- Great products
- Great experience
- Great pricing
- Simplicity

**Renewal Process:**

| Scenario | Experience |
|----------|------------|
| No changes wanted | Automatic renewal |
| Changes wanted | Self-serve changes in platform |
| Price changes | Communicated in-app + email |

**Churn Triggers:**
- Price increases
- Bad service
- Changes in plan quality/types

---

### Stage 6: Expand

**How they buy more:**
- Add more employees
- Add more products (dental, vision, life, etc.)
- Upgrade plan level

**Expansion is self-serve within the platform.**

---

### Journey Summary

| Stage | Action | Time | Experience |
|-------|--------|------|------------|
| Discover | Find Cakewalk | - | Multi-channel |
| Explore | Browse, window shop | Minutes | No commitment |
| Enroll (Employer) | Complete enrollment | Minutes | Self-serve |
| Activate (Employees) | Open enrollment | 1 week | Self-serve, guided |
| Retain | Stay & renew | Ongoing | Automatic + self-serve |
| Expand | Add more | Ongoing | Self-serve |

---

### Human Touchpoints

**Philosophy:** Self-serve first, humans for edge cases.

| Situation | Human Involvement |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Standard enrollment | None (fully self-serve) |
| Complex questions | Chat/support escalation |
| Edge cases (compliance, multi-state) | Human in the loop |
| High-value accounts | Optional white-glove |

---

### Key Metrics by Stage

| Stage | Key Metric |
|-------|------------|
| Discover | Traffic, channel attribution |
| Explore | Signup rate, browse-to-signup conversion |

---

### Journey Risks & Mitigations

**Risk 1: Drop-off during enrollment**

If employer flow has too many steps, they'll abandon.

*Mitigations:*
- Minimize required fields
- Save progress (come back later)
- Show progress indicator

**Risk 2: Low employee enrollment**

If employees don't complete enrollment, coverage fails.

*Mitigations:*
- Automated reminders
- Mobile-friendly experience
- Simple plan comparison tools

**Risk 3: Renewal churn from price increases**

Price sensitivity is high for SMBs.

*Mitigations:*
- Communicate value, not just price
- Offer alternatives (different plans)
- Early warning + proactive outreach

## Appendix: Strategic Context

### Key Strategic Insight (from GTM Evaluation)

The earlier analysis identified three GTM archetypes. Cakewalk aligns with:

**Primary: Aggregator → Abstraction → Obsolescence**
- Aggregate supply (carriers, plans)
- Standardize the experience
- Make brokers optional over time

**Secondary: Unbundled Human Layer**
- Core flow is self-serve
- Humans available on-demand for edge cases
- Humans are support, not gatekeepers

### The Broker Question

**Strategic Position:** Make brokers optional, not required.

Brokers exist because of:
- Fragmented inventory
- Opaque pricing
- Manual underwriting
- Disconnected systems

If Cakewalk solves these structurally through technology, brokers become a choice, not a necessity.
